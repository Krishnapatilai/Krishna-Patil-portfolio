Why Product Management?

A different kind of journey

I used to think the path was clear: study, get an MBA abroad, climb the corporate ladder. Business exposure, leadership, strategic decisions — that was the plan.

Then I discovered Product Management. And something clicked. Here was a space where technology met business, where empathy met execution, where curiosity was a superpower — not a liability.

Instead of waiting years to gain that exposure, I started building. I shipped features, wrote user stories, talked to users, and learned that the best insights rarely come from decks — they come from conversations.

I'm someone who goes deep on problems. Not because I always find the answer, but because the quality of your questions determines the quality of your products.

"The more I learn, the more I realize how much there is to learn. That's exactly why Product Management excites me."

Why Spotify Wins at Product

Spotify isn't just a music app. It's a personalization engine disguised as entertainment. Here's what makes their product decisions extraordinary.

  • Discover Weekly turned passive listeners into active discoverers — 40M+ users engaged in its first week
  • Personalization creates a positive feedback loop: the more you listen, the better it gets, the more you listen
  • Social features (Wrapped, shared playlists) turn product usage into cultural moments, driving organic growth
  • Free tier isn't a charity — it's the world's largest user research panel, informing every premium decision
  • Podcast integration creates a singular audio destination, reducing context-switching for users
  • Data flywheel: user behavior → better recommendations → more engagement → more data
The best products make users feel understood without ever asking intrusive questions.

Swiggy Group Ordering Feature

Ordering food for a group is still painfully broken. One person plays coordinator while others keep changing their minds. Here's how I'd fix it.

Problem Statement

When 3+ people order together, the experience fragments — screenshots, screenshots forwarded on WhatsApp, someone always forgets. The friction kills the joy of group dining.

  • Persona: College students & office colleagues ordering lunch together
  • Pain: No real-time collaboration → one person carries the cognitive load
  • Solution: Shared cart with individual selections, live updates, split-payment
  • Prioritization: RICE Score — High Reach (group orders = 35% of volume), High Impact on retention, Moderate Effort
  • Success Metrics: Group order frequency, avg. order value, re-order rate within 7 days

Beyond the Roadmap

What I'm ultimately building toward

My immediate goal is clear: become a Product Manager who ships things that genuinely improve people's lives.

But beyond product, I dream of building a restaurant someday. Not because I want to leave product. Because great restaurants and great products solve the exact same problem:

Creating experiences people want to come back to.

In a restaurant, you optimize for delight — ambiance, taste, service, memory. In a product, you optimize for value — speed, clarity, satisfaction, retention.

The craft is the same. The tools are different.

📱 + 🍽️

A product that earns daily opens.
A restaurant that earns return visits.

Both are built on the same foundation: deep empathy for the person on the other side of the experience.
